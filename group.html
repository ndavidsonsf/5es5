About the Group

A confidential space to explore what matters most.

This is a small, intimate group — just 6 men — offering a warm, affirming space to explore your inner world alongside others who understand the particular texture of gay men's lives.

Rooted in a psychodynamic and relational approach, the group goes beyond symptom relief. We explore the deeper patterns, early experiences, and relational dynamics that shape who we are and how we connect.


The group is especially supportive for men navigating:

  • Aging & life transitions
  • Chronic illness or health-related stress
  • Grief, loss, and identity changes
  • Loneliness or disconnection
  • Changes in intimacy or community
  • Questions of meaning & emotional depth

Is This For You?

You don't have to navigate this alone.

Group therapy can feel like a big step — and it is a powerful one. Many men find that the group experience offers something individual therapy cannot: the direct, lived experience of being truly known by others who understand.

This group may be especially meaningful if you've been in individual therapy and are ready to bring that inner work into relationship — or if connection and belonging feel like the deeper questions you're holding.

Stanley will meet with you individually before you join to ensure the group is the right fit and to ease any uncertainty about the process.

You might find this group especially valuable if you…

  • Feel isolated or disconnected from other gay men
  • Are navigating grief, loss, or major life change
  • Struggle with intimacy or repeating relationship patterns
  • Are dealing with aging, health, or changes in your body
  • Long for a community that truly sees you
  • Want to deepen your emotional intelligence and self-awareness
  • Are ready to move beyond just coping — toward genuine growth

Group Format

Thoughtfully structured, deeply human.

01

Small & Intimate

With just 6 members, this is a close-knit group. The small size creates the conditions for real trust, genuine depth, and meaningful connection.

02

Process-Oriented

Sessions aren't structured around topics or curriculum. Instead, what arises in the room — between members, and within each person — becomes the material for exploration.

03

Led with Care

Stanley brings his warmth, depth-psychology training, and 11 years of experience to hold the group with skill and compassion — creating safety without rigidity.
